# Table of Contents

Learning by Doing: Failure is Part of the Process

These guides do NOT hold your hand. They are designed to make you do your own research, to better understand concepts first-hand, and to learn how things can go sideways real quick and how to fix them on-the-fly.

I have built this entire knowledge base in a way that will FORCE you to learn by doing it yourself. You will encounter errors. Commands will fail. Configurations will break. That's intentional.

Remember: Failure is just a step in the way. No one has 100% success ratings or gets it right on the first shot. Trust yourself. Debug. Research. Adapt. That's how you become competent.

And always document what ever you are doing because when stuff breaks (and they will!) you can recall exactly how you fixed it.

The Concentric Circles Learning Path

Operational security follows a natural progression, like ripples expanding outward from a stone dropped in water. Start at the center with foundational concepts, then move outward through increasingly complex layers of defense. Each circle builds on the previous. Numbers indicate the recommended learning order.

1-2

Understanding & Personal Practice

The Foundation: Learn concepts and implement daily habits

/1.OpSec_101/ START HERE

Foundational operational security guides for beginners. Master these concepts before moving to technical implementations.

Part 1: Introduction to OpSec

What is operational security, why it matters, and the mindset required for effective privacy protection.

Part 2: Threat Modeling

Identify your adversaries, understand their capabilities, and build proportional defenses.

Part 3: Digital Hygiene

Daily security practices: password management, encrypted communication, browser hardening, and compartmentalization.

Why Start Here?

You cannot secure your operating system if you don't understand threat models. You cannot implement hardware security if you haven't mastered password hygiene. Technical defenses fail without foundational knowledge.
